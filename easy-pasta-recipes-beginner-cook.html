5 Easy Pasta Recipes Every Beginner Cook Should Master

📅 2026-05-25 ⏱️ 2 min read 📝 583 words

Pasta is the ultimate beginner-friendly food. It's cheap, fast, forgiving, and almost impossible to mess up. If you can boil water and stir a pan, you can make restaurant-quality pasta at home. These 5 recipes are the foundation — master them and you'll have a weeknight dinner ready in 20 minutes or less.

1. Aglio e Olio (Garlic and Oil Pasta)

The simplest pasta dish in existence — and one of the most delicious.

  • 1 lb spaghetti
  • 6 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup olive oil
  • Red pepper flakes
  • Parsley, chopped
  • Parmesan cheese

How: Cook pasta. In a large pan, slowly cook garlic slices in olive oil on low heat until golden (NOT brown — burnt garlic is bitter). Add red pepper flakes. Toss in drained pasta with a splash of pasta water. Top with parsley and parmesan.

Total time: 15 minutes. Total cost: about $3. Tastes like a $22 Italian restaurant dish.

2. Creamy Tuscan Chicken Pasta

  • 1 lb penne
  • 2 chicken breasts, sliced thin
  • 2 cups spinach
  • 1/2 cup sun-dried tomatoes
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup parmesan
  • 3 cloves garlic

How: Season and cook chicken 6 min/side. Remove. In same pan, cook garlic 30 seconds, add cream and parmesan, stir until smooth. Add spinach and sun-dried tomatoes. Add cooked pasta and chicken. Toss everything together.

💡 Pro Tip: SAVE YOUR PASTA WATER. Before you drain the pasta, scoop out a cup of the starchy water. Adding 2-3 tablespoons to your sauce makes it silky and helps it cling to the pasta. This is what restaurants do.

Simple swaps make a big difference: use olive oil instead of butter, add extra vegetables, choose whole grains over refined, reduce sugar by 25-50%, and use herbs and spices for flavor instead of excess salt.
